Research Interests

My research deals with mathematical control theory and its applications to microfluidics and micro-swimming.

Micro-Swimming

I study the motion of swimming micro-organisms through modelling, controllability analysis and numerical simulations. I use the tools of control theory on various models to better understand, predict or influence related phenomena.

Magnetic micro-robots and local controllability

I have worked on a model of magnetized elastic micro-swimmer, controlled by an external magnetic field, and studied its controllability properties around equilibria. In a more general framework, I am interested in local controllability conditions for control-affine systems.

State-constrained controllability

I take interest in the subject of state-constrained controllability of PDE systems. In particular, I am investigating nonnegative state controllability of linear and semilinear coupled parabolic systems.
